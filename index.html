Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common chemistry questions.

What is molar mass? ‚ñº

Molar mass is the mass of one mole of a substance, measured in grams per mole (g/mol). It's numerically equal to the relative atomic or molecular mass.

How do I balance chemical equations? ‚ñº

Chemical equations are balanced by ensuring the same number of atoms of each element appears on both sides of the equation. This is done by adding coefficients in front of the chemical formulas.

What is stoichiometry? ‚ñº

Stoichiometry is the calculation of relative quantities of reactants and products in chemical reactions. It's based on the conservation of mass and the concept of moles.

What are the gas laws? ‚ñº

The gas laws describe the relationships between pressure, volume, temperature, and amount of gas. The ideal gas law (PV = nRT) combines all these relationships into one equation.
